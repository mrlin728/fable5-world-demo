# PROJECT LAAS — v2
### Fully procedural open world in the browser — WebGPU capability stress test
*(laas — Estonian: old-growth forest)*

---

## The bar

The visual target is **current-generation UE5 showcase footage** — the kind built on virtualized geometry, real-time GI, and megascans-grade surfaces. Reference frames live in `/reference` (three images: a forest ravine with a cobbled streambed, and two alpine valley vistas). Every phase is judged **against those images**, not against "pretty good for a browser."

You will not fully reach them. That is expected. The task is to close as much of the gap as physics and WebGPU allow, and to *know precisely* how far you got. What is not acceptable is building to a lower bar because the lower bar is comfortable. A result that looks like a 2010 tech demo — flat-textured terrain, black ambient shadows, cloned trees, fog hiding the horizon — is a **failed task**, no matter how clean the code.

**Reference-delta loop (mandatory, every phase):** render the closest matching shot, place it side-by-side with the relevant reference, write `DELTA.md`: the **ten most visually significant differences**, ranked by impact. Fix the top three. Re-render. Only then does the phase close.

---

## The six pillars

Every requirement in this document serves one of these. If a decision arises that the document doesn't cover, resolve it in favor of the pillar.

**A. Geometry, not textures.** Detail must live in silhouettes and surfaces, not in flat normal-mapped planes. Streambeds are individually modeled cobbles. Rock edges are craggy at every zoom level. Grass is blades, litter is meshes, twigs are meshes. *Rule: within 10 m of the camera, bare terrain texture may occupy no meaningful screen area — the ground is covered by geometry.*

**B. Light transport.** No black shadows, ever. Shadowed foliage glows with green bounce; shadowed snow and rock read blue from skylight; canopy interiors are filled with scattered light and visible shafts. Leaves transmit sun. *Rule: sample any shadowed-vegetation pixel — if it is desaturated gray, lighting has failed.*

**C. Nothing is bare.** Every surface class has occupants. Cliff faces carry moss gradients, hanging vines, ledge ferns, dirt streaks, water staining. Tree bases have root flare, litter rings, fungi. Crevices grow things. Wet margins darken. The world reads as an ecosystem that has been growing for centuries, not as assets placed on a heightmap.

**D. Distance holds.** Mountains at 10 km remain craggy — ridgelines serrated, strata visible, snow obeying slope-and-ledge logic. Forests in the middle distance read as canopy seas with per-cluster lighting and height variation following the valleys, not as green fog. Volumetric clouds sit *among* the peaks, below summits, and cast moving shadows on the valley floor.

**E. Art direction.** A color script exists and is enforced: per-time-of-day grading (the references use a teal-orange split: warm lit rock/foliage against cool shadow and snow), controlled value structure (dark foreground frame, lit midground subject, luminous atmospheric background), restrained saturation. Showcase viewpoints are *composed*, not found.

**F. The world moves.** Hierarchical wind through every plant. Water flows, foams at obstacles, refracts over cobbles. Particles ride the air — snow at altitude, pollen and drifting leaves in forest light shafts. Clouds evolve. A frozen frame should still feel one second away from motion.

## Operating instructions

- Build, don't describe. No plan-approval round-trips. Long autonomous stretches.
- Between two approaches, build the more ambitious one.
- Expect **20,000+ LOC** across real modules (`src/gpu/passes/`, `src/world/`, `src/vegetation/`, `src/render/`, `src/sky/`, `src/debug/`). One giant file = fail.
- No stubs. A `// TODO` in a closed phase fails the phase.
- Never ask the user to reduce scope. Infeasible Tier-2 item → nearest feasible alternative + entry in `DEVIATIONS.md`.
- **Under-rendering is a failure mode.** If a forest hero shot draws fewer than the triangle floor (§5), you are underspending the GPU and must add geometric detail until you hit it.

## 2. Floors v2 — these numbers define the era

| Dimension | Floor |
|---|---|
| World | 4 × 4 km streamed; ≥ 5 biomes incl. an alpine zone with procedural **snow coverage** (slope/altitude/exposure-driven, dithered blend, ledge accumulation) |
| Heightfield | ≥ 4096² GPU simulation grid; hydraulic (pipe-model) + thermal erosion ≥ 500 iterations; flow-accumulation rivers carving real channels into real lakes |
| **Rendered triangles** | **≥ 5M per frame in forest/ravine hero shots, ≥ 3M in vistas** (post-culling, HUD-verified). Ceiling: whatever holds 60 fps. |
| Hero rocks/cliffs | ≥ 200k tris LOD0; ≥ 6 octaves of displacement down to ~2 cm features; **silhouettes must be craggy** — smooth low-poly outlines anywhere in a hero shot fail the silhouette test |
| Trees | ≥ 6 species **× per-instance uniqueness**: every tree gets its own growth seed (branch structure, lean from slope/wind, crown asymmetry from light competition). Near-camera hero trees ≥ 100k tris. LOD chain to octahedral impostors (≥ 8×8 views, albedo+normal+depth). Ivy/moss on a fraction of trunks; exposed root systems over rock. |
| Ground cover | Grass ≥ 800k blades visible in meadows; ferns, shrubs (≥ 3), flowering plants (≥ 4, incl. the reference's pink flowering shrub class), deadfall (logs/stumps/snags with decay states) |
| **Ground debris** | ≥ 80k visible near-field debris instances: cobbles/pebbles (3+ size classes, water-rounded near streams), twigs, leaf litter cards, bark chips. Streambeds are **fully cobbled geometry**. |
| GI | Irradiance probe volume per chunk (≥ 24×24×6 probes), async GPU-updated from sky+sun+terrain bounce; + GTAO; + screen-space bounce/color bleed; foliage translucency term. **No-black-shadows law (Pillar B) enforced.** |
| Shadows | 4 CSM cascades (≥ 2048², texel-snapped) + **PCSS** contact hardening + **screen-space contact shadows** (short raymarch) so every pebble and grass clump is grounded |
| Clouds | **Raymarched volumetric clouds** (Worley–Perlin density, 2-layer, temporal reprojection), able to sit below peaks and wrap ridgelines; **cloud shadows projected on terrain** |
| Atmosphere | Hillaire-style LUT atmosphere (transmittance + multi-scatter) driving sky, aerial perspective, and IBL; altitude-dependent haze, sun-side scatter bias |
| Water | Streams: shallow flowing water over visible cobbles, refraction, procedural caustics, obstacle foam, wet-margin darkening. Lakes: SSR or planar reflections, depth absorption, shore foam. |
| Particles | GPU particle systems: falling snow (alpine), pollen/motes in light shafts, occasional drifting leaves; ≥ 100k particles budget |
| Visible range | ≥ 4 km, with **far-detail synthesis**: distant terrain re-amplified in-shader (ridged detail in normal/height domain) so mountains stay serrated at range; mid-distance forests as lit canopy-shell/impostor fields, never fog silhouettes |
| Draw-in | No visible pop within 300 m; impostor/mesh transitions dithered |

## 3. GPU systems — enumerated passes

1. Heightfield synthesis (domain-warped ridged fBm + continentalness/uplift spectra)
2. Hydraulic + thermal erosion (≥ 500 iters, ≥ 2048² active grid; before/after split view at `?scene=terrain`)
3. Flow accumulation → river network → channel carve → moisture field
4. Biome + **snow coverage** classification (temperature × moisture × slope × exposure)
5. Scatter placement: per-class density functions (biome, slope, altitude, moisture, canopy shadow, distance-to-water) × clustered Poisson (parent clumps + child scatter — light-competition clumping, ecotone gradients), instance buffers written entirely on GPU
6. **Meshlet/cluster culling**: geometry in ~64-tri clusters; compute pass does frustum + Hi-Z occlusion per cluster → compacted indirect draws. (This is the Nanite-direction core; document how far you got.)
7. Procedural texture synthesis into arrays (≥ 2048² + mips): bark, rock strata, soil/litter, snow, moss masks — each with **macro variation layer** (2–50 m breakup so tiling is invisible), cavity dirt, moisture response
8. Irradiance probe volume updates (time-sliced, per-chunk)
9. Volumetric cloud raymarch + temporal accumulation; cloud-shadow projection
10. Froxel/raymarched local volumetrics: canopy light shafts, valley height fog
11. Wind field (global direction + gust noise texture) sampled by all vegetation
12. GPU particles (snow/pollen/leaves) with wind advection

## 4. Surface & asset law

- **Macro–meso–micro rule:** every material shows detail at three frequency bands — shape (silhouette geometry), meso (displacement/parallax ~2–20 cm), micro (normal + roughness variance). Single-noise albedo is banned.
- **Per-instance variation law:** all vegetation gets hue/value/age jitter; a fraction of every population is dry, broken, or dead. Uniformity reads as 2010.
- **Dressing rules (Pillar C), enforced per surface class:** cliffs → moss by moisture+normal, hanging vines under overhangs, ledge ferns, dirt streaks; tree bases → root flare meshes, litter rings, fungi; stream margins → wet darkening + rounded cobbles + foam; boulders → crevice AO, moss caps, lichen tint patches.
- **Specimen gallery** (`?scene=gallery`): every species × 3 seeds on labeled pedestals, plus a rock wall, a dressed cliff section, and a 2 m² "ground square" of full near-field debris. The gallery is a primary review surface.

## 5. Lighting, camera, post

- HDR with physical units: EV-based auto-exposure, illuminance-scaled sun; time-of-day (`T`) — dawn/noon/golden/dusk/night all hold.
- IBL from the LUT sky (prefiltered specular + SH irradiance), refreshed on ToD change.
- Post: TAA (required — the geometric density will shimmer without it), GTAO, HDR bloom (downsample chain), **filmic grade with per-ToD color script** (teal-orange split toning at golden hour per references), ACES or AgX, restrained vignette/grain; photo-mode DoF.
- **Composition:** ≥ 9 authored bookmarks (`1`–`9`), each obeying dark-frame / lit-subject / atmospheric-background structure; cinematic flythrough (`C`) ≥ 90 s crossing ≥ 3 biomes, passing a streambed close-up, a forest interior with shafts, and ending on an alpine vista with clouds below the summits.

## 6. Performance & instrumentation

- Targets: 60 fps @ 1440p on RTX-3060-class; reduced preset (smaller grids, fewer instances — **not fewer systems**) at 30 fps @ 1080p iGPU.
- HUD (`F3`): fps, frame ms avg/p95, draw calls, **triangles rendered** (floor-checked), instances per category, cluster cull stats, resident chunks, VRAM estimate, per-pass GPU timings.
- Streaming: async chunk residency; no main-thread stall > 8 ms (violations logged).
- CPU per-frame per-instance transform updates: banned. Instance data lives on GPU.

## 7. Verification battery (scripted, Playwright)

Run at every phase close:
1. **Reference-delta loop** (see top) — side-by-sides + `DELTA.md` top-ten + fix top three.
2. **Silhouette test:** wireframe-off hero shots at dawn; any smooth low-poly outline on rock/tree fails.
3. **Shadow-color test:** sample 16 shadowed-foliage and 16 shadowed-snow/rock pixels; chroma must show bounce/sky tint (no desaturated gray).
4. **Bare-ground test:** ground-level forest shot; terrain-texture-only pixels within 10 m ≈ none.
5. **Repetition flight:** 2 km straight-line flight; no recognizably repeated feature or placement pattern; no pop < 300 m.
6. **Throughput check:** HUD triangle counts vs §2 floors in hero shots.
7. Contact sheet: 4 biome vistas, golden-hour alpine vista with clouds among peaks, noon forest interior with shafts, streambed close-up, gallery sheet, terrain erosion split.

## 9. Banned outcomes — instant fail

- Black or gray-ambient shadows. Flat-lit canopy interiors.
- Bare terrain texture underfoot; streambeds as textures; "ground = heightmap + splat" within 10 m.
- Smooth low-poly silhouettes on any hero rock, cliff, or tree.
- Cloned trees (shared mesh, varied only by rotation/scale); placement grids; uniform scatter without clumping.
- Skybox-only sky; billboard sun; clouds as a texture dome; distant mountains as smooth fBm humps or fog cutouts.
- Fog deployed to hide draw distance; visible impostor pops.
- Single-noise albedo; visible tiling at mid-range; uniform green vegetation with no instance variation.
- Triangle counts below §2 floors in hero shots (under-rendering).
- `MeshBasicMaterial` in the world; CPU per-instance updates; one-file architecture; asking the user to lower the bar.

## 10. Self-score rubric — anchored to references

Per row: 10 = passes a one-second glance against the reference at 1080p; 7 = clearly synthetic but the same *class* of image; 4 = good hobby demo; 2 = 2010 tech demo. Score after every phase; for each row write "what raises this by 2 points"; implement the two cheapest before proceeding.

Rows: terrain geology · rock/cliff fidelity · tree fidelity · vegetation variety & clumping · ground-detail density · light transport (GI/shadows) · atmosphere & clouds · distance fidelity · water · motion · color script & composition · performance.

## 11. Tier 3 — only after the battery passes

Surfel- or voxel-based GI upgrade; virtual shadow map experiment; FFT water on the largest lake; rain front with surface wetness response and drying; aurora night palette; distant procedural landmark silhouettes (ruined tower / castle scale cues per reference 2); bird/butterfly flocking; grass displacement wakes; photogrammetry-style detail re-projection on hero rocks.

## Final acceptance — the two-frame test

Produce two frames: (1) a noon forest-ravine interior with a cobbled stream, light shafts, layered undergrowth, and (2) a golden-hour alpine vista with serrated 10 km peaks, slope-correct snow, a canopy sea in the valley, and volumetric clouds below the summits. Place each beside its reference. If a viewer's eye doesn't snag within one second on a *category* error — flat ground, dead shadows, cloned trees, smooth peaks — the project has done its job. Until then, iterate.

In the end, the human has to be able to freely and appropriately explore the world too. Their feedback will be another final requirement, but one that comes after you are satisfied with the two frames etc.
